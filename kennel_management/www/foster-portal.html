🐕 Dog Foster Care Guide

First 24 Hours

  • Set up a quiet, confined space — a crate or small room
  • Provide fresh water and food (follow shelter feeding schedule)
  • Allow the dog to decompress — don't force interaction
  • Take them outside every 2 hours for bathroom breaks
  • Keep other pets separated initially

Daily Routine

  • Feeding: Follow the schedule provided. Note any changes in appetite
  • Exercise: At least 2 walks per day (leash only for first 2 weeks)
  • Medication: Administer as prescribed — see your foster animal's details below
  • Socialization: Gradual introductions to new people and environments

When to Contact Us Immediately

  • 🚨 Not eating for 24+ hours
  • 🚨 Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 12 hours
  • 🚨 Lethargy or difficulty breathing
  • 🚨 Signs of pain (whimpering, limping, guarding)
  • 🚨 Bite incident of any kind
  • 🚨 Escape or running away

🐈 Cat Foster Care Guide

Setting Up

  • Prepare a single room with litter box, food, water, hiding spots
  • Place litter box away from food and water
  • Provide scratching surfaces and a comfortable bed
  • Cat-proof the room: secure cords, remove toxic plants

Daily Care

  • Clean litter box daily — note any changes in usage
  • Fresh water daily, feed on schedule provided
  • Spend quiet time in the room — let the cat approach you
  • Interactive play 2-3 times daily (wand toys, laser pointers)

🍼 Neonatal / Bottle Baby Guide

Critical Care Schedule

  • Feed kitten milk replacer (KMR) every 2-3 hours (including through the night for newborns)
  • Stimulate urination/defecation after each feeding using warm damp cloth
  • Keep ambient temperature 85-90°F (29-32°C) for first week, gradually decrease
  • Weigh daily on a kitchen scale — they should gain weight every day
  • Watch for fading kitten syndrome: cold, lethargic, crying → call us immediately
